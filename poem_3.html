Spare Wheel


Hey!

I don't even know if you'll respond to this,
Or even try to read it at least.
Lately we live in different worlds,
I could swear one of us turned to a beast.

A beast that attacks but remains blind,
A beast that destroys without a care.
Crushing every memory left behind,
Like love was never planted there.

They say the strong are those who never cry,
That tears are weakness men should hide.
But silence slowly learns to die,
While lies eat everything inside.

I remember how we laughed together,
Under sunny skies blessed by the weather.
Life felt lighter than a feather,
As we dreamed of forever together.

Those dreams made my whole heart happy,
Never thought our story could turn snappy.
Every glance made my body freeze,
Sending little shivers through the breeze.

My friends all said it meant something,
I was foolish enough to believe.
Till you stopped the love that held us,
And gave my heart a reason to grieve.

You used me then walked away,
Leaving wounds too deep to mend.
Pressed and salted, left to stay,
Like a story without an end.

I asked myself, "What did I do?
Why did your colours fade to blue?
How did forever lose its view?
How did I become a stranger to you?"

You never saw the tears I hid,
Because boys are taught to hide their pain.
We smile while something breaks within,
Then drown alone beneath the rain.

So I searched until I found the truth,
A lesson life would soon inspire.

You never need a spare wheel,

Unless somewhere

There's already

A punctured tire.

Suddenly every excuse made sense,
Every silence, every delay.
Every promise lost its strength,
Every "I love you" faded away.

I wasn't your forever,
I wasn't your final guy.
You said that I was your air,
I guess he became your sky.

You thought your secret was locked away,
Hidden safely inside a cave.
Until I smiled and looked your way,
And spoke the truth you tried to save.

"Don't think I was just born.

I know about you...

And John."

You expected rage,
You expected war.
You expected me
To settle the score.

But when you cheated,
You made your choice.
So I answered pain
With my own voice.

I chose peace.
I chose me.
I chose the man
I'm meant to be.

No wonder we stopped seeing eye to eye,
You couldn't even say hello.
The eyes that once looked into mine,
Had somewhere else to go.

So I pointed gently to the door,
Not because I wished you pain.
I simply knew my heart deserved
To never beg again.

I never wanted to settle the score,
I chose myself instead of war.
I know my worth,
I've known since birth.

Mother raised a gentleman,
Who fights fire like a fireman.
I don't know what you saw in the other guy,

But this much I know...

If he ever leaves,
And your whole world falls apart,

Don't come looking for the spare wheel,

When you were the one

Who threw away

The car's best part.
