A Note To Self

Hey!

This poem won't be going to you,
I guess it's just a note to self.
Time has finally shown the truth,
Like opening books left on a dusty shelf.

They've been sitting there for far too long,
Covered in memories I couldn't erase.
I got so lost inside called "love",
That I forgot my own place.

Even metal rusts with passing years,
Nothing escapes the hands of time.
Only the One above remains,
Forever steady, forever sublime.

I wished our bond could last forever,
Because every day with you felt better.
Being apart became my storm,
No man can predict tomorrow's weather.

The love we shared felt pure and true,
Every thought I had belonged to you.
Every moment carried quality,
I never knew one heart lacked honesty.

You stayed because of what I gave,
Not because you chose my soul.
Then vanished like the morning dew,
Leaving emptiness where once I felt whole.

Like water meeting summer's sun,
You disappeared without a sound.
Rose into another sky,
While leaving my heart on the ground.

When people asked what went wrong,
You pointed every finger at me.
While chasing nothing but the green,
Lost in another money tree.

Your friends believed the tale you sold,
Every lie became their game.
Their hatred carried little weight,
Because truth outlives every name.

I sensed the ending long before,
An overthinker always does.
We reached a knot too tight to keep,
Held together by what never was.

So I cut every single tie,
Because I've always hated lies.
Not just the strings that led to you,
But every mask and every disguise.

You were the rotten potato,
Hidden quietly inside the sack.
Funny how one broken soul
Can slowly turn the whole thing black.

You smiled whenever I fell low,
Thinking pain had won the fight.
But pressure never buried me,

It taught me

How flowers reach the light.

You threw your dirt to bury me,
Certain I would lose my power.

But all you did

Was help me grow

Into a wildflower.

You thought you were the greatest prize,
The sweetest taste, the perfect spice.
Now life feels lighter without your shadow,

Like a cold drink

Filled with ice.

Ever since you walked away,
The weight upon my back was gone.
Like pulling out a hidden knife,
I finally breathed and carried on.

I survived a different wildlife,
Turned shelf-life into real-life.
Lost a person,

Found myself.

And maybe

That was always

The better prize.

So here's my note
To myself,

And to anyone
Reading this.

Know your worth.

Don't stay
Where love only lives
On one side.

Because love should never feel
Like begging to be chosen.

If they cannot love you
The way you deserve,

Walk away.

Not because you stopped loving them.

But because

You finally started

Loving yourself.
